18. Similiter. The line of Vv'yclif's argument runs thus: The
act of sight, as itis elicited in our brain, gives us awareness of
the colour seen, independently of its size. Suppose this colour
to be a white surface; each point that is white goes to make
up the intensity of this awareness Now if it were really composed
of intensive parts, each point (of which there is an infinite
multitude) contributing a certain intensity to the act, this act
would be infinitely intense. To each point there would correspond
a certain intensive part. —— The adversaries would no doubt
answer that the intensity contributed by each point of the sur-
face is infinitely small.
